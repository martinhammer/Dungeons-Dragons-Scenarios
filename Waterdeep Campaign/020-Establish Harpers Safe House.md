Scenario 2 - Establish Harpers Safe House
=========================================

_“The Harpers need a safe haven from which to launch their campaign against the darkness.”_

### Components
This scenario uses components (tiles, treasures, monsters and events) from **Legend of Drizzt**.
Do not use any components from other boxes, except coin treasure cards (refer to Campaign Rules).

### Special Components in this Adventure
#### From the Legend of Drizzt box
* Start tile
* Secret Cave tile
* Jaraxle Baerne figure and villain card
* Key token

### Game set-up
Take the Secret Cave tile, and shuffle it with three random tiles. Shuffle the remaining tile deck and place these four tiles under the six tiles of the deck.

Place aside the Jaraxle Baerne figure and villain card.

Place aside the Key token.

Place the Start tile on the table and the two heroes on the tile.

Start the scenario with two healing surges.

For treasures, use the Legend and Drizzt deck mixed with coins from Dungeon of the Mad Game (and refer to Campaign Rules page).

### When you start the advendutre
Read the following:
> The Harpers need a safe haven from which to launch their campaign against the darkness. They asked you to clear out tunnels under an old house which they will use as their base.

### When you reveal the Secret Cave tile
Instead of drawing a monster card, place the Jaraxle Baerne villain.

Read the following:
> After wandering through the tunnels you have discovered a secret cave. It looks like it's a hideout of some villain, and he doesn't look at all pleased that you have located his secret shelter. He steps out of the shadow and snarls, "I don't care who you are and what you're doing here. Nobody must know about this place. Prepare to die!". With dread you realise that this is the infamous rogue and murderer Jaraxle Baerne. There is no chance to run, you must fight for your life!

### After you have defeated Jaraxle Baerne
Place the Key token on the tile where Jaraxle Baerne died.

Read the following:
> With the final blow Jaraxle Baerne is finally defeated and you can breathe a sigh of relief. You notice that he dropped a heavy key as he fell. You search his hideout but there is no door or chest into which the key fits. Even though you don't know what lock the key might open, you decide to take it with you togehter with the rest of the treasures you found. You have a feeling that it might be useful in the future.

### Victory conditions
To win this scenario, you need to defeat all monsters and Jaraxle Baerne.

You lose if a Hero starts their turn with 0 HP and no surge tokens left.
